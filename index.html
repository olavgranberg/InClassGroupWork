The reference role

Your job is to ensure that the needed literature, references and quotes are added to the project. Making the answers trustworthy and reliable. A essential part of doing academic work. Remember that how you know it, is equal as important as what you know!

The answer role

Your responsibility is making sure your groups answers is written down. Besides this you are also responsible for adding the specified tasks to the workspace. You will show what your group know and the person responsible for references will show how. Make sure the traffic light is green, then you know the minimum criteria has been reached.

The presenter role

Your role is to present the groups collaborative outcome/result. You can edit the presentation points area but only add short sentences according to bullet points format. Remember to keep your points precise and well thought-out.

The analyst role

Your role is to note findings and subjects for further discussion during the collaborative open discussions. Furthermore the analysts perspective must be critic and nuanced. You can only edit the “Question for further Discussion” Element, adding questions your group seek an answer to or find interesting to discuss. Each added point will automatically become an question, forcing you to act thoughtful and skeptical. Mark certain questions that you find valuable presenting.
